We can begin with "Please say hello and tell us where is your bedroom. Is it across from another bedroom? Is it between the bathroom and another room? Then tell use where is your home. Is it on the corner of an intersection? Is it in the middle of a street? Is it next to another building? Is it near or close to something?" For example, "Hello, my name is Joel. Our bedroom is in between the bathroom and my work room. Its door is across from the stairs. Our house is in the middle of Fessenden Street, and it is close to the Friendship Heights metro station."

Below is the dialog in the book. Let's make breakout rooms. In the main room let's repeat the dialog, then go into each room and see whether the students can identify where each building is on the map.

  • Is there a bank near here? Yes. Go straight. It's on the corner of Church Street and Third Avenue.
  • Where's the hospital? Go straight for three blocks. Turn right. It's on the corner of First Avenue and Bank Street.
  • Where's the drug store? Go to Third Avenue and turn right at the corner. Go straight for two blocks.
  • Is there a post office near here? Yes. Go straight for two blocks. Turn right at the corner. It's on the corner of Second Avenue and Bank Street.
  • Is there a school near here? Go straight for two blocks. Turn left at the corner. It's on the corner of Second Avenue and White Street.
